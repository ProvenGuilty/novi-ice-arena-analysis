## 1. Why It's For Sale

The City of Novi isn't selling because the arena is failing — they're selling because **they don't want to own the risk anymore**.

- **Built in 1998 for $11.5M** (financed with revenue bonds). The debt is now **fully paid off**.
- The city operates it as an **enterprise fund** — by law, they **cannot profit from it**. At best they break even.
- The building is **28 years old** and the mayor's own analogy is a car past 100,000 miles: *"repairs are going to become more frequent and more costly."*
- The Finance and Administration Committee began discussing the facility's future in **July 2025** and brought three options to council in March 2026:
  1. Solicit bids for sale (with potential deed restrictions)
  2. Commission additional studies ($8K–$80K range)
  3. Maintain current operations
- **Majority of council favors selling**, specifically to **Suburban Sports Group (SSG)**, the Farmington Hills company that has managed the arena since 1999 (~25 years). The mayor stated he would **only** consider an offer from Suburban.
- The city wants to reinvest sale proceeds into ITC Park, Bosco Fields, and the vacating police station property.
- Only **31% of users are Novi residents** — the city is subsidizing a regional facility.

**Bottom line: The city is getting out of the building-ownership business. They see all downside risk, zero upside.**

## 2. Why It's "Only" $4M

The $4M price tag is **not suspiciously cheap for what this is**. Here's the math:

### The Appraisal

- **Appraised value: $3.6M.** The $4M figure is the mayor's *opening negotiation number* — already above appraisal.
- **Replacement cost for a new 2-sheet facility: $37–46M** at current construction rates (~$400–486/sqft). But appraisals of *aging* single-purpose buildings don't work on replacement cost. They work on **income approach** and **depreciated value**.

### Why the Value Is Low Relative to Original Cost

- **75,000 sqft of single-purpose building.** If the ice business fails, this building is nearly worthless. You can't easily convert it to retail, housing, or offices. The slab has miles of refrigeration piping embedded in it.
- **Revenue: ~$2.2M/year** (FY2024-25). But that's *gross* revenue. After operating expenses (staff, utilities, insurance, Zamboni maintenance, management fees to Suburban), this thing **breaks even at best**. The city explicitly says they cannot profit.
- **At $4M purchase price with $2.2M revenue, you're looking at a ~1.8x revenue multiple.** For a break-even business with massive CapEx risk, that is **market rate or even generous**.

### Energy Costs

- **Electricity for refrigeration: $3,200–$25,000+/month** per sheet, depending on season and efficiency.
- Annual utility bills for a 2-sheet facility can easily reach **$150K–$300K+**.

### The CapEx Time Bomb

| Component | Lifespan | Age | Est. Replacement Cost |
|-----------|----------|-----|----------------------|
| Refrigeration system (2 sheets) | 20–30 years | **28 years** | $500K–$1.4M |
| Dasher boards (per rink) | 15–25 years | 28 years | $100K–$250K each |
| Concrete slab/piping (per rink) | 25–35 years | 28 years | $150K–$400K each |
| Zamboni machines (x2) | 10–15 years | Unknown | $150K–$250K each new |
| HVAC/dehumidification | 15–25 years | 28 years | $200K–$500K |
| Roof (75,000 sqft) | 20–30 years | 28 years | $300K–$750K |

> **A realistic 10-year CapEx reserve for this facility is $2–4M. That effectively doubles your purchase price.**

## 3. Reasons This Would Be a BAD Investment

### A. You'd Be Competing Against the Incumbent

- **Suburban Sports Group** has managed this facility for **25 years**. They know every customer, every league, every coach.
- SSG already owns/operates **4 other ice facilities** in metro Detroit (Farmington Hills, Rochester, Macomb; sold East Lansing in 2023).
- SSG runs the **largest Learn-to-Skate program in the country** (recognized 2020–2022).
- SSG runs **HB Hockey Club**, which has produced Stanley Cup champions and Olympians.
- The mayor publicly stated he would **only consider an offer from Suburban.** An outside buyer would need to convince council to go against their expressed preference.
- If you buy it and hire someone else to manage, you lose all institutional knowledge. If you self-manage, you're running a **7-day/week, 16-hour/day operation**.

### B. Youth Hockey Association Concerns

- The **Novi Youth Hockey Association** president publicly expressed concern that Suburban would push out NYHA in favor of their own HB Hockey programs.
- If *Suburban* buying it worries them, an unknown buyer would cause even more anxiety.
- NYHA has **200+ members** and is a core revenue driver. Losing them would be catastrophic.

### C. Industry Consolidation — And It's Getting Ugly

- **Black Bear Sports Group** (PE-backed, Florida-based) now owns **9 rinks in Michigan** and **47 nationally**. They're buying aggressively.
- The **Michigan Attorney General is actively investigating Black Bear** for *"potential anticompetitive and unfair trade practices"* — price gouging families after acquiring local monopolies.
- This means: (1) PE money is flooding the space and you'd compete with deep-pocketed consolidators, and (2) the regulatory environment is getting hostile toward rink owners who raise prices.

### D. The Economics Are Brutal for an Independent Owner

- **Break-even is the ceiling**, not the floor, based on the city's own experience.
- As a private owner you'd now owe **property taxes** (~$40K–$80K/year) that the city didn't pay.
- **Insurance: $50K–$100K+/year** (slip-and-fall, hockey injuries, Zamboni incidents).
- **Staffing**: Skilled ice technicians working nights, weekends, and holidays in freezing conditions. Chronic industry shortage.
- **Seasonality**: Even indoor rinks see significant summer demand drop-offs.

### E. Emotional Attachment Is a Risk Factor

- Nostalgia is not a business plan. Every failed acquisition case study has a chapter about the buyer who *"couldn't let it go."*
- The real test: **Would you buy this building at this price if you'd never been there before?**

### F. Deed Restriction Risk

- Council is discussing **deed restrictions** requiring it remain an ice arena, plus **first right of refusal** on resale.
- This would **destroy your exit options**. You couldn't pivot to another use, and you couldn't sell freely if things go sideways.

## 4. The (Very Thin) Bull Case

- **700,000 visitors/year** — real, proven foot traffic
- **Two NHL-regulation sheets** — versatile programming capability
- **Trained Olympians, NHL players** — genuine prestige and brand value
- **Novi is wealthy** — median household income ~$100K+, strong youth sports culture
- **Michigan is a hockey state** — deep demand floor
- **Replacement cost is 10x purchase price** — buying well below replacement
- **Regulatory scrutiny on PE consolidators** (Black Bear) could benefit a community-oriented independent owner

## 5. Capital-Raising Plan

*Only relevant if due diligence clears all red flags above.*

### Phase 1: Due Diligence ($10K–$80K)

Before raising capital, spend money to understand what you're buying:

| Item | Est. Cost |
|------|-----------|
| 20-year capital needs assessment | $8–10K |
| Building/mechanical inspection (refrigeration, HVAC, roof, slab) | $15–25K |
| Environmental Phase I (ammonia/refrigerant contamination) | $3–5K |
| Operational/financial audit (Suburban's actual P&L) | $10–25K |
| Legal review (deed restrictions, zoning, lease structure) | $5–10K |

### Phase 2: Financing Structure

**Total capital need: ~$5–6M** ($4M purchase + $1–2M reserves)

| Source | Amount | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **SBA 504 Loan** | $2.4–3M | 25-year term, ~6.5% rate. SBA favors community recreation facilities. Requires 10–15% down. |
| **Your equity** | $400–600K | 10–15% down payment. Skin in the game is non-negotiable. |
| **Community investment group / LPs** | $500K–1M | 5–10 local families/investors who care about the rink. Structured as LLC with managing member. |
| **Seller financing from City of Novi** | $500K–1M | The city may carry a note — they want the arena to survive. |
| **CapEx reserve line of credit** | $500K–1M | Separate credit facility for inevitable mechanical failures. |

### Phase 3: Operational Model

- **Option A**: Hire Suburban to continue managing (if willing — they may decline if they wanted to buy)
- **Option B**: Hire an experienced rink GM ($80–120K/year) and build your own operations team
- **Option C**: Form an operator partnership with a smaller regional rink management company

### Phase 4: Revenue Enhancement (Years 1–3)

As a private owner, you *can* profit — unlike the city. Target **5–10% net margin** ($110–220K/year).

- Expand adult leagues, tournament hosting, summer camps
- Add off-ice training facility, improved concessions, party/event packages
- Negotiate favorable utility contracts; LED lighting upgrades; DTE energy efficiency grants
- Explore **naming rights** for sheets/arena (local businesses)
- Corporate event packages and school field trip programs

### Key Numbers to Validate Before Proceeding

- [ ] Actual annual operating expenses (not just revenue)
- [ ] Condition and remaining life of the refrigeration system
- [ ] Annual utility costs (could be $200K+)
- [ ] Suburban's management contract terms and expiration date
- [ ] Specific deed restrictions the city will require
- [ ] Property tax assessment as a private commercial property
- [ ] Insurance quotes for the facility

## 6. Final Verdict

**This is a high-risk, low-margin, operationally intensive business with massive hidden CapEx exposure, and you'd be trying to outmaneuver a 50-year-old industry incumbent that the city explicitly prefers to sell to.**

Emotional connection to the rink is real and valid — but it's also the #1 factor that could lead to a bad financial decision.

### Alternative Paths Worth Considering

- Join a community advisory board for the arena
- Help organize/lead the Novi Youth Hockey Association
- Negotiate an operational role under whoever buys it
- Invest as a minority LP in a Suburban-led acquisition

### Minimum Next Steps (If Still Serious)

1. **FOIA the enterprise fund financials** — full P&L is public record
2. **Commission an independent mechanical inspection** of the refrigeration system
3. **Consult a commercial real estate attorney** about deed restriction implications
4. **Talk to Suburban** — understand if they'd welcome a partner or view you as competition
5. **Talk to the Novi Youth Hockey Association** — understand their concerns and whether they'd support your ownership
